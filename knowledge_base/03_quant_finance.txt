## **I. Black-Scholes Model**
Used for **option pricing**:

\[
C = S_0 N(d_1) - Xe^{-rt} N(d_2)
\]

where:
- **C** = Call Option Price
- **S_0** = Current Stock Price
- **X** = Strike Price
- **r** = Risk-Free Rate
- **N(d1), N(d2)** = Cumulative Distribution Functions.

## **II. Monte Carlo Simulation**
- Used for **derivative pricing and VaR (Value at Risk).**
- Simulates thousands of random stock price movements.

## **III. Risk Metrics**
- **Sharpe Ratio** = Measures **risk-adjusted return**.
- **Beta** = Measures **systematic risk**.
