## **I. Introduction to Market Analysis**
Market analysis is the foundation of financial decision-making. Investors use **fundamental** and **technical analysis** to evaluate securities and predict price movements.

## **II. Fundamental Analysis**
Fundamental analysis assesses a company's **intrinsic value** using financial statements, industry trends, and economic conditions.

- **Financial Metrics**
  - **Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio**: Measures valuation by comparing stock price to earnings per share (EPS).
  - **Earnings Per Share (EPS)**: Indicates a company's profitability per share.
  - **Debt-to-Equity Ratio (D/E)**: Evaluates financial leverage.
  
- **Economic Indicators**
  - **Gross Domestic Product (GDP)**: Measures economic growth.
  - **Consumer Price Index (CPI)**: Indicates inflation trends.
  - **Unemployment Rate**: Signals labor market health.

## **III. Technical Analysis**
Technical analysis uses historical price patterns to forecast movements.

- **Indicators**
  - **Moving Averages (SMA, EMA)**: Smooths price data.
  - **Relative Strength Index (RSI)**: Measures overbought (above 70) and oversold (below 30) conditions.
  - **MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)**: Identifies trend reversals.

## **IV. Real-World Example**
Example: Apple Inc. (AAPL) has a **P/E ratio of 24**, suggesting fair valuation relative to earnings.
